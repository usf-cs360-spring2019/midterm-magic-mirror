Visualization

Interpretation

  • Summary: Shows the most common calls that the San Francisco Fire Department received in January 2019.
  • Interpretation: This visualization shows the major call types which are Medical incident, Alarms, Struncture Fire,and Traffic Collision. These four call types are totally more than 92% of all calls. Consequently, in the next visualizations we put our focus on these four major incidents.
    visualization 2
    visualization 3
  • Interactivity: There are two Interactivity in this visualization, by hovering mouse over the pie chart you can see the name and number of incidents for the selected part. Also by selecting a legend color, the related part of chart to that color will be highlighted and also the other parts of graph fade in the background.

Visualization

Interpretation

  • Summary: Parallel Coordinate that shows average response time in January 2019 considering three different dimentions which are Priority of calls, Group Type and Call Type. Although there is a axis showing the Group Types, colors are based on the Group Type which is not conveying new information but helps the readability of the graph. Users can follow and observe average response time(last axis) according to these dimentions.
  • Interpretation: This visualization contains a lot of information. We can follow the average response time according to different priorities or observe how fast different gropus reacting to different kind of incidents, for instance magority of Traffic Collisions are catogorized as priority 3 additionally the gropus probably exprience traffic before arriving on the scene, thus their average response time is among the highest.
  • Interactivity: By mouseover any line the path related to that line will be highlighted, also the average response time according to that path will be displayed in the white box next to the last axis.
